## Problems & Pain Points
**Core problem:** Business is growing but the technology isn't keeping up. They're using spreadsheets, manual processes, generic software that doesn't fit, or nothing at all. They know they need better systems but don't know where to start and don't have anyone to build it.
**Why alternatives fall short:**
- Freelancers (Fiverr, Upwork): Hit or miss quality. No ongoing relationship. Once the project is done, you're on your own.
- AI-generated sites ($300-$600): Generic, no support, breaks, no one to call. Looks like what it is.
- Big agencies: $10K-$50K+ for a website alone. Layers of account managers. Slow.
- DIY platforms (Wix, Squarespace): Look like templates. Locked into their platform. $400+/year in hidden fees. Break when you try to customize.
- Off-the-shelf software (Salesforce, HubSpot): Expensive, complex, designed for enterprise. Overkill for a 20-person company.
**What it costs them:** Hours of manual work per week, missed leads, unprofessional online presence, inability to scale, stress.
**Emotional tension:** "I know I need this but I don't know who to trust. Last time I spent money on tech it was a waste."

## Competitive Landscape
**Direct:** Local digital agencies — often charge premium but deliver templated work, pass you off to junior devs, slow turnaround.
**Secondary:** Freelance marketplaces (Fiverr, Upwork) — cheap but unreliable, no ongoing relationship, you get what you pay for. AI website builders — cheap but generic, no support, no strategy.
**Indirect:** DIY platforms (Wix, Squarespace, GoDaddy) — seem affordable but lock you in, look generic, hidden costs add up. Off-the-shelf SaaS (Salesforce, HubSpot, Zoho) — powerful but complex, expensive, not customized to your workflow.
